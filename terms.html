What LuMe is

LuMe is a light metering tool for photographers. It uses your device's camera sensor to measure ambient light and calculate suggested camera exposure settings. It is intended as a reference aid — not a substitute for professional judgment.

LuMe's readings are estimates based on your device's sensor. Actual exposure results may vary depending on your camera, lens, and shooting conditions.

What's free, what's Pro

LuMe is free to download and use. The core features — matrix and center-weighted light metering, the flash calculator, the depth-of-field calculator, exposure compensation, ND filter compensation, reciprocity correction, the Zone System view, and the viewfinder overlays (grid, histogram, clipping warning, EV badge) — are available to all users at no cost.

LuMe Pro is an optional upgrade that unlocks additional features. As of this writing, Pro unlocks spot metering, custom camera profiles, and LiDAR-based subject distance (the latter only on devices that include a LiDAR sensor). The exact set of Pro features may change over time.
